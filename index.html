Biography

Brandon Sanderson (born December 19, 1975) is an American fantasy and science fiction writer. He is best known for the Cosmere universe, in which most of his fantasy novels (most notably the Mistborn series and The Stormlight Archive) are set. He is also known for finishing Robert Jordan's epic fantasy series The Wheel of Time. Sanderson was raised in Lincoln, Nebraska before attending Brigham Young University, where he received degrees in English literature and creative writing. In 2008 Sanderson started a podcast with author Dan Wells and cartoonist Howard Tayler called Writing Excuses, involving topics about creating genre writing and webcomics.

The Cosmere

The Cosmere is the universe that the majority of the books Brandon Sanderson has written is set in. It is a vast galaxy consisting of many worlds. The underlying mythology of the Cosmere revolves around Adonalsium, a power of creation who was broken into 16 pieces, or shards. these shards made the hosts effectively become gods, however theese shards were fragments of Adonalsium's own personality, so whoever hosted a shard would started to take on the aspects of that shard. for example, someone who too up the Honour shard would become more honourable. There are 12 known planets in the Cosmere, 8 of these are shardworlds, 4 either do not contain a shard of Adonalsium or it is unknown if one resides on it. These worlds are:

The idea behind the cosmere was that it was not meant to be an obvious thing tying the books together, rather it was subtle connections, reocurring characters and very small hints that there was this connected universe.
